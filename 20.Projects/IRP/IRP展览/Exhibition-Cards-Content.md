## 📌 CARD 1 — The Problem

> *A5 landscape / portrait. Place near the project entry point.*

**TITLE:**
```
Exercise has Strava.
Sleep has Oura.
Eating well has... nothing.
```

**BODY:**
```
When you complete a run, you feel it — endorphins,
distance, a social score. When you sleep well, Oura
confirms it with a number you check first thing every
morning.

But when you eat a genuinely nutritious meal?
You feel full. That is all.

The metabolic benefits of good nutrition play out over
weeks at a cellular level entirely invisible to experience.
This is not a motivation problem.

This is a feedback problem — and design has never solved it.
```

```
70% of users abandon dietary tracking apps within 2 weeks.
30-day retention: 3%.   — Business of Apps, 2024
```

## 📌 CARD 2 — The Ethical Constraint

> *A5. Pair with the Intuitive Eating design constraints image.*

**TITLE:**
```
Any feedback system must liberate,
not restrict.
```

**BODY:**
```
Tutor feedback (April 2026) sharpened the central
ethical question of this project: intensive dietary
self-monitoring is associated with elevated disordered
eating risk in a significant sub-population.
                            — Roth et al., 2024

This project adds a non-negotiable design constraint:

  Any dietary feedback system must support
  Food Freedom — the right to eat without
  guilt, compulsion, or surveillance.

The designer's role is not to optimise.
It is to genuinely care.
```

**KEYWORD STRIP:**
```
Compassion-Based Design  ·  Embodiment  ·  Empowerment  ·  Observer Perspective
```
