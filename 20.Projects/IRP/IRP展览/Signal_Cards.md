# 📡 Signal Cards

> [!abstract] Emerging trends and practices shaping the future of dietary feedback.

## 🃏 Signal 1: CGM for Non-Diabetics
**The Shift to Real-Time Metabolic Feedback**
> Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM) is transitioning from a medical device to a consumer wellness product.
- **Data:** Global OTC CGM market reached **$370.7M in 2024**, with non-diabetic users forming the largest segment (**41.46%**).
- **Signal:** The technology for real-time biological feedback exists, but the *ethical design* framework to prevent obsessive monitoring is still missing.

## 🃏 Signal 2: The Anti-Diet Movement & Intuitive Eating
**Amplifying Internal Body Attunement**
> Moving away from external dietary rules toward internal body signals (hunger, fullness, satisfaction).
- **Data:** High baseline Intuitive Eating scores correlate with a **74% reduction in binge eating risk** over 8 years.
- **Signal:** Design must foster **Embodiment** (connecting users deeply with their own bodies) rather than imposing external metrics.

## 🃏 Signal 3: Social Rituals as Feedback
**Cultural Counter-Models to Tracking**
> Feedback does not need to be numerical or technological; it can be cultural and experiential.
- **French Paradox:** Low food anxiety through structured, pleasure-oriented social rituals (e.g., *déjeuner*).
- **Japanese Itadakimasu:** A designed pre-meal transition creating an embodied loop of gratitude and pause.
- **Signal:** Effective dietary feedback can operate seamlessly through social and cultural channels.
