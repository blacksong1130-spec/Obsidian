### 【Part 2】导师今天给的3个核心框架 · 1:30–5:00

> **【此时展示：白板/纸上写3个词】**
> 1. COMPASSION-BASED DESIGN
> 2. EMBODIMENT + EMPOWERMENT
> 3. OBSERVER PERSPECTIVE

*"The first thing my tutor said was: this is a compassion-based project. That word — compassion — reframes everything.*

我的导师说的第一句话是：这是一个基于同情心的项目。“同情心”这个词重塑了一切。

*Most health technology is built around optimisation. Track more, measure more, improve more. My tutor pushed back on that. She said: the question isn't 'how do we make people eat better?' The question is 'how do we help people feel better about eating?' That shift — from optimiser to steward — is what compassion-based design means. The designer isn't judging the user. The designer is genuinely caring for them.
*
大多数健康技术都是围绕优化构建的。追踪更多、测量更多、改进更多。我的导师对此提出了异议。她说：问题不是“我们如何让人们吃得更好？”问题是“我们如何帮助人们在饮食方面感觉更好？”从优化者到守护者的这种转变，正是基于同情的设计的含义。设计师不是在评判用户，而是真正地关心他们。

*The second framework is embodiment and empowerment. How does a design intervention actually connect people more deeply with their own bodies — not as objects to be tracked, but as subjects to be understood? And how does it give people agency over that information? The key word here is choice. Not compulsion. The system offers you something to see — and you decide whether to look.*

*The third one is the observer perspective — which is almost philosophical. Rather than reacting emotionally to an experience — 'I'm cold, this is terrible' — you adopt the position of an observer: 'seven-degree water is moving across my body.' Applied to eating, it means: instead of judging whether a meal was good or bad, you notice what you actually felt. Curiosity, not evaluation.*


第二个框架是具身性和赋权。设计干预如何真正让人们与自己的身体建立更深刻的联系——不是将身体视为被追踪的对象，而是作为需要被理解的主体？它又如何赋予人们对这些信息的掌控权？这里的关键词是选择，而非强制。系统提供给你一些可看的内容——而你决定是否去看。

*Put these three together and you get a very specific design brief: build something that makes eating feel meaningful — not by adding surveillance — but by helping people notice what's already there."*

第三个是观察者视角——这几乎带有哲学意味。与其对某种体验做出情绪化的反应——“我好冷，这太糟糕了”——你不如采取观察者的立场：“七度的水正流过我的身体”。应用到饮食上，这意味着：你不是去评判一顿饭是好是坏，而是留意自己实际的感受。是好奇，而非评判。


将这三点结合起来，你就会得到一个非常具体的设计要求：打造一种能让饮食变得有意义的东西——不是通过增加监控，而是通过帮助人们留意已经存在的事物。

### 【Part 3】文化证据：两个案例 · 5:00–8:00

**【展示：Itadakimasu 图片】**

*"My tutor gave me two cultural examples that I want to share because they perfectly illustrate the direction.*

- **以 (i)**
    
- **哒 (da)**
    
- **哒 (da)**
    
- **奇 (ki)** —— 这里的“ki”发音接近“七”
    
- **嘛 (ma)**
    
- **斯 (su)** —— 最后的“su”发音很轻，舌尖抵住下齿龈，轻轻吐气即可，不要发成沉重的“苏”。

*The first is Japanese — Itadakimasu. Before every meal in Japan, there's this ritual word: いただきます. It translates roughly as 'I humbly receive,' and it's addressed simultaneously to the food, the people who grew it, the people who cooked it, the animals involved. It takes about two seconds. But those two seconds do something crucial: they mark the beginning of eating as a significant moment. Not just fuelling up — a transition.*

*That's not technology. That's culture. But it functions exactly like feedback design — it creates intentionality before eating, not anxiety about eating."
*
我的导师给了我两个文化实例，我想分享一下，因为它们完美地诠释了这个方向。
第一个是日语——いただきます。在日本，每餐之前都有这样一个仪式性的词语：いただきます。它大致翻译为“我恭敬地领受”，同时是对食物、种植食物的人、烹饪食物的人以及相关动物说的。这个过程大约需要两秒钟。但这两秒钟起到了至关重要的作用：它们将进食的开始标记为一个重要时刻。这不仅仅是为了填饱肚子——而是一种转变。
那不是技术，那是文化。但它的作用和反馈设计完全一样——它在进食前创造意图，而不是对进食产生焦虑。

**【展示：Rozin 数据 — 可以直接念数字】**

*"The second example is the French paradox — something I actually went and researched in depth this afternoon.*

*Rozin and colleagues published a cross-cultural study in 1999 — published in Appetite journal — comparing food attitudes in the US, France, Japan, and Belgium. The finding was striking: French participants showed the strongest association between food and pleasure, and the lowest levels of food-related anxiety. Americans showed the exact opposite — most likely to modify diet for health, least likely to describe themselves as healthy eaters — despite the fact that Americans report the highest health consciousness.*

*So you have a culture — France — that treats eating as ritual, as social, as aesthetic — and produces less anxiety, not more. That's powerful for this project. It tells me that the most effective dietary 'feedback' might not be a number on a screen. It might be a ritual, a social context, an aesthetic experience.*

*Both of these point to the same design insight: feedback doesn't have to be technological to be transformative."*

第二个例子是法国悖论——实际上我今天下午还专门深入研究了这个问题。
罗津及其同事于1999年发表了一项跨文化研究（发表在《食欲》杂志上），比较了美国、法国、日本和比利时的饮食态度。研究结果令人震惊：法国参与者表现出食物与愉悦感之间的最强关联，以及与食物相关的焦虑程度最低。美国人则恰恰相反——尽管美国人声称自己的健康意识最强，但他们最有可能为了健康而改变饮食，却最不可能称自己为健康饮食者。
有这样一种文化——法国文化——它将饮食视为一种仪式、一种社交活动、一种审美体验，而且带来的焦虑更少，而非更多。这对这个项目来说意义重大。它告诉我，最有效的饮食“反馈”可能不是屏幕上的数字，而可能是一种仪式、一种社交情境、一种审美体验。
*这两者都指向同一个设计洞见：反馈不一定非得借助技术才能具有变革性。*

#### 🔺 图1：Futures Triangle（未来三角）· 8:00–10:00

**【展示：手画未来三角】**

画法（30秒）：
```
画一个等边三角形。
顶角写：PULL（拉力）
左下角写：PUSH（推力）
右下角写：WEIGHT（阻力）
```

三角内容（写在对应位置旁边）：
```
PULL（顶部，往上的力）：
"Eating well feels as rewarding as finishing a run"
= CGM消费化 / Quantified Self 2.0 / 预防医学愿景

PUSH（左下，现在正在发生的力）：
传感器成熟 / 反节食文化兴起 / 公共卫生压力
→ CGM市场：$370.7M，年增长16.9%

WEIGHT（右下，阻止改变的力）：
进食障碍伦理风险 / 用户留存崩溃（70% 2周内放弃）
食物=燃料的主流叙事 / 食品行业利益
```

**讲稿：**

*"The first Design Futures tool I'm using is called the Futures Triangle. It's developed by futures scholar Sohail Inayatullah, and it asks a deceptively simple question: what forces are shaping this particular future?*

*You have three forces in tension.*

*The Pull at the top is the vision — the future we want to move toward. For this project, that's a world where eating well feels as meaningful and rewarding as completing a run. Not because we've added more data, but because design has made the invisible biology of good nutrition finally legible. The emerging technology is already signalling this: continuous glucose monitors that were medical devices two years ago are now consumer products. The market grew to $370 million last year and is growing at 17% annually. Non-diabetic users — people choosing this for wellness — are already the largest segment at 41%.*

*The Push at the bottom-left is what's already happening, right now, that's moving us in that direction: sensor technology maturing, the anti-diet movement growing, public health systems under pressure to find preventive solutions rather than reactive treatment.*

*And the Weight at the bottom-right is what's holding us back. This is where my project is different from most health tech work. I'm not pretending the Weight doesn't exist. The biggest weight is the eating disorder risk: intensive dietary self-monitoring is clinically associated with elevated disordered eating risk in a significant sub-population. That constraint is non-negotiable for my design.*

*My project sits in the tension between these three forces. The design challenge is to strengthen the Pull, leverage the Push, and — crucially — design explicitly around the Weight."*
*我正在使用的第一个设计未来工具叫做未来三角。它由未来学者苏海尔·伊纳亚图拉开发，它提出了一个看似简单实则不然的问题：是什么力量塑造了这个特定的未来？*
你有三个力处于拉伸状态。
顶层的牵引力是愿景——我们想要迈向的未来。对于这个项目而言，那是一个吃得好与跑完一次长跑同样有意义、有回报的世界。这并非因为我们增加了更多数据，而是因为设计让良好营养的无形生物学原理终于变得清晰易懂。新兴技术已经在发出这样的信号：两年前还是医疗设备的连续血糖监测仪，如今已成为消费产品。该市场去年增长到3.7亿美元，并且每年以17%的速度增长。非糖尿病用户——为了健康而选择使用的人群——已经是最大的细分市场，占比41%。
左下角的推动力是目前正在发生的事情，它正推动我们朝着那个方向前进：传感器技术日益成熟，反节食运动不断发展，公共卫生系统面临着寻找预防性解决方案而非被动治疗的压力。
右下角的“负担”才是阻碍我们的因素。这就是我的项目与大多数健康科技工作的不同之处。我不会假装“负担”不存在。最大的“负担”是饮食失调风险：在相当一部分人群中，严格的饮食自我监测在临床上与饮食失调风险增加有关。这一限制在我的设计中是不可协商的。
***我的项目处于这三种力量的张力之中。设计的挑战在于强化拉力，利用推力，并且——至关重要的是——围绕重量进行明确的设计。**

#### ⭕ 图2：Futures Wheel（未来轮）· 10:00–11:45

**【展示：手画未来轮】**

画法（30秒）：
```
中心圆：写 "Dietary feedback remains invisible"
第一圈（4个气泡，用直线连出去）：
→ "70% users quit in 2 weeks"
→ "No emotional resonance"
→ "Behaviour doesn't change"
→ "Design energy goes elsewhere"

第二圈（从第一圈再延伸，每个气泡延伸1-2个）：
→ 从"70% quit" → "Chronic disease risk rises"
→ 从"Design energy" → "Sleep/exercise get all the design attention"
→ 从"Behaviour" → "Only privileged access personalised nutrition"
```

**讲稿：**

*"The second tool is the Futures Wheel. It's an analytical tool that maps consequence chains — what happens if the current situation continues unchanged?*

*I put at the center: dietary feedback remains invisible. That's the status quo. Now I trace the consequences outward.*

*First order: 70% of dietary tracking app users abandon within two weeks. There's no emotional resonance. Behaviour doesn't change. And because users abandon, product investment moves elsewhere — to exercise, to sleep, to areas with better retention metrics.*

*Second order: Chronic disease risk continues rising because the one behaviour most strongly linked to long-term health — dietary choice — is the one that design has most completely failed. The gap between exercise tracking sophistication and nutrition tracking sophistication widens further. And personalised nutrition becomes something only people who can afford clinics and personal dietitians can access.*

*The Futures Wheel isn't about being pessimistic. It's about making the cost of inaction visible. If nothing changes, the consequence isn't just 'people eat slightly worse.' The consequence is a widening health equity gap, a medicalisation of what should be a universal daily experience, and a design field that declared nutrition too hard and moved on.*

*My project is a response to that wheel. It asks: what if we interrupt it?"*

第二个工具是未来之轮。它是一种分析工具，用于描绘后果链——如果当前状况持续不变会发生什么？
我把核心问题归结为：饮食反馈仍然不为人知。这就是现状。现在我来探究其向外延伸的后果。
第一个问题：70%的饮食追踪应用用户会在两周内放弃使用。缺乏情感共鸣，行为没有改变。而且由于用户流失，产品投资转向其他领域——运动、睡眠以及留存指标更好的领域。
二阶问题：慢性病风险持续上升，因为与长期健康关联最为紧密的行为——饮食选择——恰恰是设计最彻底失败的领域。运动追踪的精细程度与营养追踪的精细程度之间的差距进一步拉大。而个性化营养成为只有负担得起诊所和私人营养师服务的人才能享有的东西。
未来之轮并非是悲观的体现。它旨在让不作为的代价变得显而易见。如果一切照旧，后果可不只是“人们的饮食稍有变差”。后果是健康公平差距不断扩大，本应是普遍日常体验的事物被医疗化，以及设计领域宣称营养问题太难而选择放弃。
我的项目是对那个轮子的回应。它提出了一个问题：如果我们打断它会怎样？

#### ⬛ 图3：2×2 Polarity Mapping / Scenario Matrix · 11:45–13:30

**【展示：手画2×2矩阵（即 v1讲稿中的图B）】**

画法（30秒，已有图B）：
```
纵轴：High User Agency（上）↔ System Control（下）
横轴：Low-Tech Embedded（左）↔ High-Tech Biometric（右）

四个象限：
左上：🌱 Intuitive Technology
```

**讲稿（更新版 — 加入 compassion framing）：**

*"The third tool is the Polarity Mapping — this is where I generate my three 2035 scenarios.*

*I chose two axes. The horizontal axis runs from low-tech embedded environments — think cultural rituals, social dining — to high-tech biometric tracking — continuous metabolic monitoring. The vertical axis runs from high user agency — the user chooses what to engage with — down to system control — the system decides and delivers.*

*This creates four quadrants. Three of them become my scenarios. The top-right is the High-Tech Biometric scenario: non-invasive continuous metabolic monitoring, real-time glucose and inflammation data, personalised to you. Powerful, but needs to be designed with the eating disorder constraint front and centre.*

*The bottom-left is the Cultural and Social Ritual scenario: forget the screen. Design the dining environment itself — the table, the ritual, the social context — so that eating well becomes a shared, celebrated act. Closer to Itadakimasu than to MyFitnessPal.*

*The top-left is the Intuitive Technology scenario: AI that works to amplify your body's own signals rather than replacing them with external metrics. The technology helps you hear yourself more clearly. That's the compass-based design principle in action.*

*The bottom-right quadrant — system control plus high technology — is the warning case. That's the surveillance future. That's what most current health tech is quietly building toward. That's what this project explicitly refuses."*

第三个工具是极性映射——这是我生成三个2035情景的地方。
我选择了两个坐标轴。水平轴从低技术嵌入式环境（想想文化仪式、社交聚餐）延伸到高技术生物特征追踪（持续代谢监测）。垂直轴从高用户自主性（用户选择参与的内容）向下延伸到系统控制（系统决定并执行）。
这就形成了四个象限。其中三个象限成为了我的情景设定。右上角是高科技生物识别情景：非侵入式连续代谢监测、实时血糖和炎症数据，且为你量身定制。功能强大，但在设计时必须将饮食失调限制置于首要和核心位置。
左下角是文化与社交仪式场景：忘掉屏幕。设计用餐环境本身——餐桌、仪式、社交情境——让好好吃饭成为一种共享、值得庆祝的行为。更接近“いただきます”（我要开动了），而非“我的健身伙伴”（MyFitnessPal）。
左上角是直观技术场景：AI致力于增强你身体自身的信号，而不是用外部指标取代它们。这项技术能帮助你更清晰地感知自身。这就是基于指南针的设计原则在发挥作用。
右下角象限——系统控制加上高科技——是警示案例。那是监控的未来。那是当前大多数健康科技正在悄然构建的方向。而这正是本项目明确拒绝的。

### 【Part 5】今日调研：案例 + 数据 · 13:30–16:00

**【无需图，直接说数字即可】**

*"This afternoon I went deep on three things that directly inform the Design Futures work.*

*First: the Karl Krukow case study. Krukow is a behaviour designer — he's actually been a guest lecturer on this tutor's course. He worked with COOP, the European grocery retailer, to redesign the in-store choice environment for climate-friendly purchasing. Using point-of-sale nudges, space management, and environmental cues — no apps, no tracking, no data dashboards — they achieved: 14% CO₂ reduction in six months, exceeding their 2030 targets by five years. 25% increase in sales of climate-friendly products. 60% reduction in food waste.*

*What this tells me: environmental design at the decision point is more powerful than individual motivation. That's directly applicable to dietary feedback design. The question isn't 'how do I give people more data?' The question is 'how do I redesign the moment of eating?'*

*Second: I verified the intuitive eating data. There's an eight-year longitudinal study — the EAT 2010-2018 study — that tracked over 1,500 young adults. The finding: participants with high intuitive eating scores at baseline showed a 74% reduction in binge eating risk over eight years. 74%. Not marginal. And this protects across five dimensions: binge eating, restrictive behaviour, body dissatisfaction, self-esteem, and depression.*

*This is the empirical backbone of the compassion-based design principle. Designs that amplify internal signals — rather than imposing external metrics — are not just ethically preferable. They're better for health outcomes.*

*Third: I looked at the Stanford design futures project on eating disorders and the Future of Food 2050 project from Emilia Ziolek. Both use mixed methods — primary research, observation, speculative prototyping — that map directly onto my methodology. Particularly Ziolek's approach: she built a hydroponic prototype, did direct kitchen observation, and combined it with case study analysis. That's the model I want to follow for my speculative artefacts."*

今天下午，我深入研究了三件直接影响设计未来工作的事情。
首先：卡尔·克鲁科夫（Karl Krukow）案例研究。克鲁科夫是一位行为设计师——实际上他曾是本课程的客座讲师。他与欧洲食品杂货零售商COOP合作，重新设计了店内的选择环境，以促进气候友好型采购。通过使用销售点提示、空间管理和环境提示——不使用应用程序、不进行跟踪、不使用数据仪表盘——他们取得了以下成果：在六个月内将二氧化碳排放量减少了14%，比2030年目标提前了五年。气候友好型产品的销售额增长了25%。食物浪费减少了60%。
==这告诉我：决策点的环境设计比个人动机更有影响力。这直接适用于饮食反馈设计。问题不是“我如何给人们更多数据？”问题是“我如何重新设计进食时刻？”==
第二：我核实了直觉性饮食的数据。有一项为期八年的纵向研究——EAT 2010 - 2018研究——跟踪了1500多名年轻人。研究发现：基线时直觉性饮食得分高的参与者在八年内暴饮暴食风险降低了74%。74%，可不是个小数字。而且这在五个维度上都有保护作用：暴饮暴食、限制性饮食行为、身体不满、自尊和抑郁。
这是基于同情的设计原则的实证支柱。放大内部信号（而非强加外部指标）的设计不仅在伦理上更可取，而且对健康结果也更有益。==？？？==
*第三：我研究了斯坦福大学关于饮食失调的设计未来项目，以及埃米莉亚·齐奥莱克（Emilia Ziolek）的《2050年食物的未来》项目。这两个项目都采用了混合方法——包括基础研究、观察、推测性原型制作——这些方法与我的研究方法直接对应。尤其是齐奥莱克的方法：她制作了一个水培原型，进行了直接的厨房观察，并将其与案例分析相结合。这就是我想在我的推测性人工制品研究中遵循的模式。*
