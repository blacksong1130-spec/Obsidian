## 📜 完整讲稿

> **使用方法：** 括号里是"此时展示哪张图"，不用背，看着说就行。用英文讲。

### 【Part 1】开场 · 0:00–1:30

**【展示：图A 三行对比表】**

*"I want to start with a very simple question.*

*When you go for a run — you feel it. Endorphins, sweat, your Strava segment time. When you sleep well — you see it. Your Oura score tells you how recovered you are.*

*But when you eat a genuinely nutritious meal — what do you feel? You feel… full. That's it. Nothing else. No signal. No score. No feedback.*

*My project is about that gap. Why does eating well feel like nothing — and what would it take to change that through design?"*

---

### 【Part 2】问题核心 · 1:30–4:00

**【展示：Fogg B=MAP 图】**

*"Most people assume this is a motivation problem. People know they should eat better — they just don't do it. So we build apps that track calories and count macros.*

*But the Fogg Behaviour Model tells us something different. Behaviour happens when motivation, ability, and prompt align at the right moment. Current dietary tools fail on all three simultaneously.*

*High effort to log every meal — low ability. No metabolic signal until weeks later — weak prompt. No emotional resonance — declining motivation.*

*This isn't a willpower problem. This is a design failure. A fundamental failure to translate invisible biology into legible signal."*

---

### 【Part 3】对比：什么被设计解决了 · 4:00–6:00

**【展示：Oura 界面截图 + Strava 截图】**

*"Compare this to exercise and sleep. Strava's genius isn't GPS accuracy — it's the social layer. A segment leaderboard turns any stretch of road into a competition. Oura doesn't just measure sleep — it gives you one number every morning that distils invisible biology into something emotionally legible.*

*These tools work because they close the feedback loop. They make invisible physiology immediately visible, emotionally resonant, and socially shareable.*

*Dietary behaviour, by contrast, is what I'm calling a design desert. MyFitnessPal — the dominant tool — was launched in 2005 and hasn't meaningfully changed. 70% of users abandon it within two weeks. The interface barely evolved in twenty years.*

*The question is: why has nutrition been left behind?"*

---

### 【Part 4】学科定位 · 6:00–8:00

**【展示：Mermaid 学科定位图（Proposal Section 4）】**

*"This project sits at the intersection of four fields.*

*Behaviour Design gives me the framework for why current tools fail — Fogg, Thaler, Nudge Theory.*

*Speculative and Critical Design — Dunne and Raby — gives me the methodology to prototype futures that don't exist yet.*

*Futures Studies gives me the temporal orientation — specifically backcasting from a preferred 2035 to identify present design decisions.*

*And Food Studies and Health Psychology give me the critical constraint that makes this project different from most health tech work: you cannot design dietary feedback without confronting the ethical risk of disordered eating.*

*That constraint is what makes this project unique. Most tools optimise food. This project asks: how do we design feedback that supports food freedom — not restricts it?"*

---

### 【Part 5】三个核心研究洞见 · 8:00–10:30

**【展示：文献推演链 Canvas 截图】**

*"My literature review produced three clear findings.*

*First: the feedback gap is structural, not motivational. Nutrition's benefits operate at subcellular timescales — weeks and months — completely invisible to conscious experience. This is not solvable by more information or better motivation. It requires a design solution.*

*Second: this is genuinely risky territory. The research is unambiguous — intensive dietary self-monitoring is associated with elevated disordered eating risk in a significant sub-population. Any feedback system that doesn't design for this will cause harm.*

*And third: the most powerful feedback is emotional, not numerical. Strava works not because it's accurate, but because it produces pride. Oura works not because it measures correctly, but because the score feels meaningful.*

*This tells me the design direction. Whatever I build needs to make eating well feel meaningful — in the moment, not statistically, not eventually."*

---

### 【Part 6】文化案例 — 今天导师刚提的！ · 10:30–12:30

**【展示：Itadakimasu 图片】**

*"My tutor pointed me toward two cultural references today that I find really compelling.*

*The first is the Japanese pre-meal ritual — Itadakimasu. Before eating, Japanese tradition involves this word that simultaneously means 'I humbly receive' and expresses gratitude to every element that contributed to the meal. It creates a designed moment of transition — a pause that frames eating as significant.*

*This isn't technological. It's cultural. But it functions as feedback design — it produces intentionality before eating, not anxiety about eating."*

**【展示：简单的法美对比数据（你可以直接念数字，不需要图）】**

*"The second is the French paradox. Research by Rozin — 1999 — found that French participants showed the highest association between food and pleasure, and the lowest levels of food-related anxiety. Americans showed the opposite: most likely to modify diet for health, least likely to describe themselves as healthy eaters.*

*What this suggests is that the feedback in French food culture isn't numerical — it's social, sensory, ritualistic. And it works.*

*Both of these point toward the same design insight: feedback systems don't need to be on a screen. They can be cultural, embodied, relational."*

---

### 【Part 7】我的方法论 · 12:30–14:30

**【展示：三角色 Mermaid 图（Proposal Section 4）】**

*"My approach is Design Futures — specifically three roles.*

*First, cartographer of the present: I map why current dietary feedback tools look the way they do, what assumptions they encode, whose interests they serve.*

*Second, critical provocateur: I design speculative artefacts that make visible the contradictions in current health technology. Not solutions — provocations. Objects that make you ask whether the future we're building is the one we actually want.*

*Third — and this is where my tutor's feedback today was very clarifying — preferential futurist. I'm backcasting from a specific preferred future: a world where eating well feels as meaningful and rewarding as completing a run, without producing anxiety or compulsion.*

*This project also works from what my tutor today called compassion-based design. The question isn't 'how do we make people eat better?' It's 'how do we help people feel better about eating?' That reframe changes everything about what I'm designing."*

---

### 【Part 8】三个2035场景 · 14:30–16:30

**【展示：图B 2×2 情景矩阵（手画）】**

*"I'm building three alternative futures for dietary feedback in 2035.*

*The High-Tech Biometric scenario: non-invasive continuous metabolic monitoring is ubiquitous. You can see in real-time how your lunch affected your glucose, inflammation markers, and gut microbiome — without breaking the skin. The technology exists in prototype form now. The design and ethical questions remain.*

*The Ambient Social scenario: feedback is distributed across shared dining environments and social rituals rather than individual devices. Imagine the dinner table redesigned to make eating well a shared, visible, celebrated act — closer to a Strava leaderboard than a nutrition label.*

*The Intuitive Technology scenario: AI works to amplify your body's own signals rather than replace them with external metrics. The system makes you more aware of how food makes you feel — not to track you, but to help you hear yourself more clearly.*

*These aren't predictions. They're provocations. Their job is to defamiliarise our assumptions about what health technology should look like."*

---

### 【Part 9】我现在在哪 + 我的问题 · 16:30–20:00

**【展示：Research Timeline Gantt（Proposal Section 5）】**

*"In terms of where I am: secondary research is complete. I'm moving into primary research now — starting with informal conversations with peers about their relationship with health tracking and their body. This is in active progress.*

*I have three questions for the group.*

*One: Does the ethical constraint — designing for food freedom, not against it — feel like a genuine design challenge to you, or does it feel like it narrows the project too much?*

*Three: My tutor today introduced the framing of embodiment — this idea that the design should help people connect more deeply with their own bodies, not just track them externally. Does that resonate with any of your own experiences of health tracking?"*
