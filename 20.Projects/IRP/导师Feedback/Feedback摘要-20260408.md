### 2. 💪 Embodiment & Empowerment Framing *(NEW FRAMEWORK)*
### 2. 具身与赋权框架 *(新框架)*

> *"It's kind of from the perspective of like empowerment and embodiment. How is this design intervention like empowering people with information if they so choose... but also how can you use this intervention as a way for people to actually connect more with their bodies and with their intention."*

> *"Maybe framing it from those perspectives... yes, you can be an observer in this process, but also be a part of it. Consider how choice plays a role in that, how empowerment around knowledge is right, and then how does this intervention connect us more with our body."*

**Three key dimensions to frame the design intervention:**
**设计介入的三个关键维度：**

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Choice** 选择 | Users choose how to engage with information/data |
| **Empowerment** 赋权 | Knowledge empowers rather than compels |
| **Embodiment** 具身 | Connects people more deeply with their own bodies |

**The Observer Perspective / 观察者视角:**
- Rather than being immersed in emotional reaction (e.g., "I'm so cold, this is bad"), one can adopt an observer view: "7-degree water is moving across my body."
  不沉浸于情绪反应中（如"好冷，好难受"），而是采用观察者视角："7度的水流经我的身体。"
- Applied to eating: observe the process of eating mindfully, without judgment or compulsion.
  应用于饮食：正念地观察进食过程，不带评判与强迫。

**→ Action: Add embodiment/empowerment as a framing lens in Sections 4 and 5.**
**→ 行动：在第4、5节中加入具身与赋权框架。**

### 3. 🌸 Cultural References *(NEW EXAMPLES)*
### 3. 文化参考案例 *(新例证)*

#### 🇯🇵 Japanese Pre-Meal Ritual (Itadakimasu / いただきます)
> *"From the culture from Japan, before they eating, they have like some word to say — 'itadakimasu' — to celebrate and like have food. If I have this kind of process, maybe it's inspiration for our IRP."*

- Pre-meal gratitude rituals as a **designed cultural feedback moment** — the act of saying "itadakimasu" marks the beginning of eating as something worthy of celebration and attention.
  餐前感恩仪式作为一种**设计化的文化反馈时刻**——"いただきます"将进食的开始标记为值得庆祝和关注的事情。
- Implication: can design create **ritualised feedback moments** around eating that are celebratory rather than evaluative?
  启示：设计能否围绕饮食创造**仪式化的反馈时刻**，使其具有庆典性而非评估性？

#### 🇫🇷 French vs. 🇺🇸 American Food Culture
> *"Americans associate food with health and show French show pleasure and show lowest [anxiety]."*
> *"I just wanna have more deep research from French — what's the strict food structure from French and the fat rate for the French people."*

- Tutor confirmed this as an **interesting case study** worth deeper investigation.
  导师确认这是一个值得深入研究的**有趣案例**。
- Why do French people have lower food anxiety despite (or because of?) a stricter food culture and higher fat intake?
  为什么法国人的饮食焦虑更低，尽管（或者正因为）他们的饮食文化更严格、脂肪摄入更高？
- Reference: Rozin et al. (1999) — **expand this in Section 2c**.
  参考：Rozin等人（1999）——**在第2c节中扩展此内容**。

### 4. 🔬 Primary Research Guidance 初级研究指导

> *"A good starting point for conducting primary research is sort of asking yourself: what do I need to find out that I cannot find out through secondary research?"*

> *"Starting point of like with your peers in class, talking about what their current methods are of tracking their health tracking... that could be a really gentle place to start."*

**Framework for deciding what to research primarily:**
**决定初级研究内容的框架：**

1. What is missing from secondary research? → **Emotional/phenomenological aspects of eating**
   二手研究缺失什么？→ **饮食的情感/现象学层面**
2. What population is under-researched? → **Futures perspective on dietary feedback**
   哪个群体研究不足？→ **饮食反馈的未来学视角**
3. Start gently: **informal conversations with peers** about their health tracking relationship
   从温和处开始：与同学进行关于**健康追踪关系**的非正式对话

**→ Action: Add peer conversations as initial phase in Section 5b research design.**
**→ 行动：在第5b节研究设计中加入与同学对话作为初始阶段。**
