# 🧠 02 — Eating Disorder Prevalence & Tracking Risk Data
# 🧠 02 — 进食障碍流行率与追踪风险数据

> [!quote] Claim Supported / 支撑的原文论断
> *"Roth et al. (2024) found significant associations between intensive dietary tracking and elevated disordered eating risk in adults."*

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ How to Read These Statistics / 如何理解这些数据
>
> **Three types of measurement are used in the literature — they are NOT interchangeable:**
> **文献中使用三种不同的测量方式——三者不可互换：**
>
> | Measurement Type | Definition | Example Tool |
> |-----------------|------------|-------------|
> | **Clinical diagnosis** 临床诊断 | Assigned by trained clinical raters using ICD-10 diagnostic criteria; requires structured clinical review | ICD-10 review by clinical raters (NHS surveys) |
> | **Screened positive** 筛查阳性 | Score above threshold on a validated screening questionnaire; indicates *risk*, not confirmed disorder | SCOFF questionnaire (5 questions) |
> | **Any disordered eating behaviour** 任何饮食失调行为 | Broad behavioural definition; includes subclinical patterns such as skipping meals, emotional eating, chronic restriction | Self-report behavioural surveys |
>
> **Each statistic below specifies which type is being measured.**
> **以下每一项数据均明确注明使用的是哪种测量方式。**

## 📊 Section 2: UK Adults — Screening Positive Rate (NOT Clinical Diagnosis)
## 📊 第二部分：英国成人 — 筛查阳性率（非临床诊断）

> **Source / 来源:** NHS England, *Adult Psychiatric Morbidity Survey (APMS): Survey of Mental Health and Wellbeing, England, 2023/24*
> **Measurement / 测量方式:** SCOFF questionnaire — a validated 5-question screening tool. A score of ≥2 out of 5 indicates a *possible* eating disorder. This is a screening result, **not a clinical diagnosis**.
> SCOFF问卷——一种经过验证的5题筛查工具。≥2分表示*可能*存在进食障碍。这是筛查结果，**不是临床诊断**。
> **Population / 调查人群:** Adults aged 16 and over living in private households in England
> **Published / 发布时间:** 2025 (covering survey period March 2023 – July 2024)

**9.1% of adults in England screened positive for a possible eating disorder in 2023/24**, using the SCOFF questionnaire — up from 6.4% in 2007.
**2023/24年，英格兰9.1%的成人在SCOFF问卷筛查中呈阳性（可能存在进食障碍）**，高于2007年的6.4%。

> ⚠️ **Important distinction / 重要区分:** The 9.1% figure means 9.1% of adults scored ≥2 on the SCOFF screening questionnaire — indicating they *may* have an eating disorder and warrant further clinical assessment. It does not mean 9.1% of adults have a confirmed clinical diagnosis.
>
> 9.1%意味着9.1%的成人在SCOFF筛查中得分≥2——表明他们*可能*存在进食障碍，需进一步临床评估。这不意味着9.1%的成人已获得临床确诊。

> 📌 **Source:** [NHS Digital — Adult Psychiatric Morbidity Survey 2023/24, Chapter 13: Eating Disorders](https://digital.nhs.uk/data-and-information/publications/statistical/adult-psychiatric-morbidity-survey/survey-of-mental-health-and-wellbeing-england-2023-24/eating-disorders)

## 📊 Section 4: Global Prevalence — Two Estimates and What They Mean
## 📊 第四部分：全球流行率 — 两个估计数字及其含义

> **Why two global figures exist / 为何存在两个全球数字:**
> Different organisations use different case definitions, producing substantially different totals.
> 不同机构采用不同的病例定义，产生了差异显著的总数。

| Estimate 估计数 | Figure 数字 | Definition Used 使用的定义 | Source 来源 |
|----------------|------------|--------------------------|------------|
| **Conservative 保守估计** | **~14 million** | Clinically diagnosed eating disorders only (ICD-10/DSM-5 criteria); includes anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge eating disorder | WHO, 2019 |
| **Broad 广义估计** | **~70 million** | Includes subclinical and diagnosable disordered eating behaviours beyond formal ICD-10/DSM-5 categories | NEDA (National Eating Disorders Association), cited in multiple 2023–24 sources |

**Global ED prevalence increased by 124% between 2000 and 2018.**
**全球进食障碍患病率在2000年至2018年间增长了124%。**

## 🔗 Relevance to IRP / 与IRP的关联

| Claim in Proposal / 提案中的论断 | Data Supporting It / 支撑数据 | Status / 状态 |
|--------------------------------|------------------------------|--------------|
| Intensive dietary tracking linked to elevated ED risk in adults | Roth et al. (2024) — directly cited | ✅ Confirmed |
| Eating disorders affect a significant minority, especially young women | 20.8% of women aged 17–19 clinically diagnosed (NHS 2023) | ✅ Confirmed |
| ED risk is a non-negotiable ethical constraint on any dietary feedback design | Hospital admissions +84% (2015–2021); 9.1% adults screening positive (2023/24) | ✅ Confirmed |
| ED risk is higher in adolescents than adults | 12.5% (17–19) vs. 9.1% screened positive (adults) | ✅ Confirmed |

> [!info] Design Implication / 设计含义
> Any dietary feedback system must be tested for eating disorder risk before deployment. The 20.8% clinical rate among young women aged 17–19 means the highest-risk demographic is precisely the group most likely to engage with health tracking apps. This is the ethical constraint that differentiates compassion-based design from conventional health optimisation design.
>
> 任何饮食反馈系统在部署前都必须经过进食障碍风险测试。17-19岁年轻女性中20.8%的临床确诊率意味着风险最高的群体恰恰是最可能使用健康追踪应用的人群。这正是将基于关怀的设计与传统健康优化设计区分开来的伦理约束。
