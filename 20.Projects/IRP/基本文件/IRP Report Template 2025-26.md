## AI, Referencing and Plagiarism

This is your research, your brain, your writing. Programmes to improve writing are acceptable but the use of AI to generate new texts is not acceptable for this report. The work must demonstrate your original thinking and your voice. If AI plays a role in your project, please review the RCA’s recommendations and reflect on your use in the statement of authorship and methodology section. [https://intranet.rca.ac.uk/support/it-services/using-generative-ai-at-the-royal-college-of-art/](https://intranet.rca.ac.uk/support/it-services/using-generative-ai-at-the-royal-college-of-art/)

Academic conventions must be observed. All sources including visuals must be acknowledged and accurately cited through proper quotations and in-text citations. Every sentence where you have used an idea or fact that is not your own (i.e. something you learned through your research) must include the source(s) at the end of that sentence. Direct quotations must be in quotation marks and also include the page number. Sources must appear in the reference list at the end. Use the Harvard Referencing System. The RCA library provides a guide to help you with this process - [https://rca.libguides.com/harvard/intro](https://rca.libguides.com/harvard/intro)

Plagiarism will not be tolerated. Everything must be written in your own words. If you copy text directly from another source, it must be in quotation marks and it must be introduced in your own words. The best practice is to paraphrase with limited quotations. You can read more about plagiarism in points 6 and 7 of the Student Academic Misconduct Policy: [https://rca-media2.rca.ac.uk/documents/4._Student_Academic_Misconduct_Policy_Revised_2022.pdf](https://rca-media2.rca.ac.uk/documents/4._Student_Academic_Misconduct_Policy_Revised_2022.pdf)

[[1]](#_ftnref1) What is the difference between ‘approach’ and ‘methodology’ in the context of design futures research? We are using the term ‘approach’ to refer to the sub-disciplines (sub-fields) of design and futures. These are flexible and conceptual ways of thinking, dealing with and looking at design futures projects - a perspective, theory, philosophy, or strategy (see diagram of design futures approaches in [Angheloiu et al., 2020](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/epub/10.1177/1946756719844050)).  ‘Methodology’ is a set of organised and rigorous scientific methods, tools, techniques, principles and rules. Approach sets an overall intention, values and direction of a project or practice, whereas methodology is procedural and outlines how to execute that direction.
