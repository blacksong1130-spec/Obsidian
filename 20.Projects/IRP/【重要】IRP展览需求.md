**  
Deadline: Tuesday, 14 th April**

**9:00 - 10:00 Set-up** 

All students must set up their IRP Proposal exhibition-style displays in DF studio (space allowance: 3 projects will be displayed on 2 desks)

**10:00 - 15:30 Exhibition Style Critique**

Each student must attend 2 sessions [based on the schedule](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/13OcxeVM_cedeeotJ8PK2U3e7DnoHxTaN5n0SwHeLKVk/edit?usp=sharing) - these are 1) your Formative Assessment time slot (column H) and 2) the Peer Review slot (column KLM) for an assigned student. You can remain in the class to listen to feedback others receive.

**1) Formative Assessment**

You will present the latest version of your IRP proposal in an exhibition-style format. Your work should be communicated through curated materials displayed in the space, allowing tutors and peers to engage with your project through conversation and exploration. You will discuss your project with tutors and peers who are unfamiliar with your work, and give feedback to others. 

Scheduled 15-minute Formative Assessment:

- 5-minute presentation: you will give us a short presentation of your IRP proposal using your tabletop display materials
    
- 10-minute discussion: you will receive formative feedback from two tutors and discuss it
    

Format: Tabletop display and informal presentation (**important:** please ensure to record photographs and notes of the displayed materials and feedback from tutors and peers, as you will need to include in the IRP Proposal Report submission) 

**Deliverables:** Display materials as described in [section 5. Critique in the brief](https://moodle.rca.ac.uk/pluginfile.php/337186/mod_resource/content/1/Unit%20Brief%20IRP%20Proposal%202025-26.pdf):

- **First draft of your report** (A 4, printed) following the IRP Report Template and including whatever content you have developed so far. You are encouraged to design the layout and visually communicate your work.    
    
- **Hero image with project title and your name** (A 3, printed). ‘Hero’ is used here as a metaphor for something that wins someone's attention and represents your project. It must be your original work.
    
- **3-d object representing your topic**. Can be the same as you showed March 24.
    
- **5 different postcards** or similar (printed) to be used as conversation prompts. Must include:
    
    - images
        
    - project title
        
    - research question(s)
        
    - problem framing
        
    - key research insights
        
    - overview of methodology 
        
    - any other information from the report that you wish to discuss in the critique 
        
- **Anything else** you feel is necessary to communicate and get feedback on your proposal. If you are exploring your topic through methods such as making, collecting materials or artefacts, experimenting with diegetic prototypes, or other forms of inquiry, you are strongly encouraged to include these in your exhibition.
    

**2) Peer Review**

You will visit the display of one of your assigned peers and provide a critical peer review you will [record using this template.](https://moodle.rca.ac.uk/pluginfile.php/337799/mod_resource/content/1/IRP%20Proposal_Formal%20Peer%20Review%20.docx) Once you complete it, take a photo of it and **submit it on Moodle by 6 pm on 14 th April**. **Hand a copy of the complete form to the student** you are assessing on the same day.

**Ethics** 

You must submit your ethics checklist to your tutors **by 12:00 Monday, 13 th April**. If you intend to conduct research during Spring Break, then you also need to discuss this with your tutor as soon as possible. [Read the ethics slides on Moodle](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1e8XAxMH6K6QlPBZ6STyUqodrpLzWYKaZxIZI3OIVDNo/edit?slide=id.g3076466ce8a_0_8#slide=id.g3076466ce8a_0_8) and prepare a draft of your consent form and any interview questions or other materials you need.
