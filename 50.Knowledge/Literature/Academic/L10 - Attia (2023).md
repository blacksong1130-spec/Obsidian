# L10 — Attia & Gifford (2023): Outlive: The Science and Art of Longevity

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Attia, P. and Gifford, B. (2023) *Outlive: The Science and Art of Longevity*. New York: Harmony Books.

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** Attia's **Medicine 3.0** framework argues that preventive medicine has failed because it waits for disease before intervening. The book advocates for radically earlier, more personalised intervention across four longevity pillars: exercise, sleep, **nutrition**, and mental health. Attia devotes extensive attention to metabolic health (insulin sensitivity, blood glucose regulation) as the primary dietary lever for long-term health.

**So What:** *Outlive* establishes the macro context for this project: dietary behaviour is not a lifestyle choice but a **critical health infrastructure problem**. The failure to design good dietary feedback tools is not merely inconvenient — it contributes to the chronic disease burden that Medicine 3.0 is trying to prevent. This frames the project's significance at a public health level.

**Now What:** Use Medicine 3.0 and healthspan thinking as the macro justification frame in Section 1 and Section 7. Attia's emphasis on CGM as a personal metabolic feedback tool supports the technical direction of the speculative prototyping work. Connects to [[P3 - Levels Health (2019)]] as the current commercial embodiment of Medicine 3.0 dietary thinking.

## 🔑 Key Arguments & Design Implications

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Medicine 3.0 vs 2.0** | 2.0: treat disease after onset · 3.0: prevent disease decades before onset |
| **Four Longevity Pillars** | Exercise · Sleep · **Nutrition** · Mental Health |
| **Nutrition as Infrastructure** | Dietary behaviour is as critical as healthcare systems — currently underdesigned |
| **CGM Advocacy** | Real-time glucose monitoring as personal metabolic feedback tool |
| **Healthspan vs Lifespan** | Quality of years matters as much as quantity — eating well = feeling well now |
| **Strength** | Mainstream, high-profile framing; makes nutrition urgency legible to general audience |
| **Limitation** | Primarily biomedical lens; less engagement with food culture, joy, or social eating |
| **Project Application** | Macro frame: "This project is a design contribution to Medicine 3.0's nutrition gap" |
