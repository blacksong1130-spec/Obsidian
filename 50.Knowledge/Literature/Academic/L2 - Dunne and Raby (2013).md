# L2 — Dunne & Raby (2013): Speculative Everything

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Dunne, A. and Raby, F. (2013) *Speculative Everything: Design, Fiction, and Social Dreaming*. Cambridge, MA: MIT Press.

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** Foundational text defining speculative design as a methodology for using design fiction and hypothetical artefacts to critique the present and imagine alternative futures. Dunne and Raby distinguish between **affirmative design** (solving problems within existing paradigms) and **critical design** (questioning the paradigms themselves).

**So What:** The dietary feedback space is dominated by affirmative design — apps that try harder to count calories. This project requires a critical design lens to ask whether calorie counting is even the right framework. *Speculative Everything* provides the methodological and philosophical grounding for that shift.

**Now What:** Use as the basis for the speculative prototyping methodology in Section 5. Their concept of 'design as dialogue' is particularly relevant — the artefacts produced are intended to provoke conversation, not prescribe solutions. Also connects to [[P4 - Dunne and Raby Placebo Project (2001)]] as practical precedent.

## 🔑 Key Concepts & Design Implications

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Core Distinction** | Affirmative Design (solve within paradigm) vs. Critical Design (question the paradigm) |
| **Key Concept** | Design as a tool for social dreaming and cultural critique, not just problem-solving |
| **Key Strength** | Provides rigorous methodological vocabulary for speculative work |
| **Key Limitation** | Speculative work risks disconnection from real-world constraints and deployment |
| **Design as Dialogue** | Artefacts open questions; they don't answer them |
| **Project Application** | Dietary feedback prototypes should defamiliarise calorie-counting assumptions, not just improve them |
