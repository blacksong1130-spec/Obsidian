# L6 — Roth et al. (2024): Weight-Related Self-Monitoring & Eating Disorder Risk

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Roth, A.M., Gruber, J.R., Reif, A., Pfarr, J.K. and Bauer, I.G. (2024) 'Exploring weight-related self-monitoring as a potential risk factor for eating disorder symptoms in adults: a systematic review and meta-analysis', *Appetite*, 202, p. 107610. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.appet.2024.107610 **[待核实]**

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** A systematic review and meta-analysis examining the relationship between weight/dietary self-monitoring (including app-based tracking) and eating disorder symptoms in adults. Finds **significant positive associations** between intensive tracking and disordered eating behaviours in certain sub-groups.

**So What:** This is the central evidence for the ethical constraint driving this project's design approach. The literature does not merely suggest tracking can be unhelpful — it establishes that for a significant sub-population, intensive tracking is **actively harmful**. This makes the food freedom dimension non-optional in any proposed feedback design.

**Now What:** Use the risk factors identified here to develop design exclusion criteria — specific patterns a safe dietary feedback system must avoid. Connects to [[L3 - Tribole and Resch (2020)]] as the constructive counterpoint, and to [[P3 - Levels Health (2019)]] as a real-world example where these risks manifest.

---

## 🔑 Key Findings & Design Implications

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Core Finding** | Intensive dietary self-monitoring associated with elevated eating disorder risk in adults |
| **Risk Sub-group** | Particularly those with pre-existing body image concerns or perfectionism |
| **Harmful Design Patterns** | Calorie counting · Weight logging · Food scoring systems · Punitive notifications |
| **Study Type** | Systematic review + meta-analysis (high evidence quality) |
| **Strength** | Provides quantitative evidence for ethical constraint — not just anecdotal concern |
| **Limitation** | Association ≠ causation; direction of effect unclear for some sub-groups |
| **Design Exclusion Criteria** | No weight-centric metrics · No "failure" notifications · No obsessive precision signals |
| **Project Application** | Every design prototype must be evaluated against this risk profile |
