# L7 — Kahneman (2011): Thinking, Fast and Slow

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Kahneman, D. (2011) *Thinking, Fast and Slow*. New York: Farrar, Straus and Giroux.

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** Kahneman's synthesis of decades of behavioural economics research distinguishes **System 1** (fast, automatic, emotional, present-biased) and **System 2** (slow, deliberate, rational, future-oriented) thinking. Most everyday decisions — including most food choices — operate in System 1. The book documents cognitive biases including **hyperbolic discounting** (overweighting present rewards over future ones) and loss aversion.

**So What:** Dietary behaviour fails at the System 1/2 interface. People *rationally* want to eat better (System 2) but make impulsive, present-biased choices at the moment of eating (System 1). Effective dietary feedback must therefore operate at the **System 1 level** — making the healthy choice feel immediately rewarding, not requiring deliberate calculation.

**Now What:** Use System 1/System 2 framing to evaluate speculative feedback prototypes: does this design work in the fast, automatic register? Does it require effortful cognition? The most successful designs (Strava's leaderboard, Oura's morning score) operate at System 1 — this project should aim to do the same.

---

## 🔑 System 1 vs System 2 — The Dietary Design Table

| | System 1 (Fast) | System 2 (Slow) |
|---|---|---|
| **Speed** | Instant, automatic | Deliberate, effortful |
| **Emotion** | Emotional, intuitive | Rational, analytical |
| **Time orientation** | Present-biased | Future-oriented |
| **Food decisions** | ✅ Most real decisions happen here | ❌ Where dietary goals live |
| **Hyperbolic discounting** | "This pizza tastes great NOW" | "Healthy eating benefits in 6 months" |
| **Design implication** | Feedback must work here — fast, felt, emotional | Current calorie apps only reach System 2 |

| Overall Assessment | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Strength** | Foundational; widely validated; explains intention-action gap precisely |
| **Limitation** | System 1/2 is a simplification; actual cognition is more nuanced |
| **Project Application** | Design test: "Can a System 1 thinker use this feedback in 3 seconds without effort?" |
