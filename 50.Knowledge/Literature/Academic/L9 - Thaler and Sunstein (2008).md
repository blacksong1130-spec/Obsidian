# L9 — Thaler & Sunstein (2008): Nudge

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Thaler, R.H. and Sunstein, C.R. (2008) *Nudge: Improving Decisions About Health, Wealth, and Happiness*. New Haven, CT: Yale University Press.

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** Thaler and Sunstein's framework of **libertarian paternalism** argues that the design of choice architectures — how options are presented — can predictably influence behaviour without restricting freedom. Their nudge interventions leverage cognitive biases (default effects, social norms, salience) to steer people toward better choices while **preserving autonomy**.

**So What:** Nudge theory provides a middle path between punitive dietary rules and pure behavioural free-for-all. The design challenge of this project — making eating well feel good *without* coercing it — is essentially a nudge design problem. The principle that feedback should work *with* human psychology rather than against it runs throughout the project.

**Now What:** Apply nudge design principles (defaults, salience, social norms, feedback timing) to the speculative prototyping phase. The central question: what nudge architecture could make eating well feel as socially salient and immediately rewarding as exercise? Connects to [[L1 - Fogg (2009)]] on prompts and [[L7 - Kahneman (2011)]] on System 1 design.

## 🔑 Nudge Principles & Dietary Design Applications

| Nudge Principle | Mechanism | Dietary Design Application |
|----------------|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Default effects** | People go with defaults; make healthy the default | Pre-populate healthy meal options; default to positive framing |
| **Social norms** | People follow what "most people do" | Show how peers eat well; social validation for healthy choices |
| **Salience** | Prominent information influences choice | Make nutritional benefit immediately visible at point of decision |
| **Timely feedback** | Feedback closest to action is most effective | Feedback within minutes of eating, not hours |
| **Loss framing** | Losses feel worse than equivalent gains | "You missed today's nutrition window" — but use cautiously re: eating disorder risk |
| **Hyperbolic discounting** | Future rewards need to feel present | Translate long-term metabolic benefits into immediate emotional rewards |

| Overall Assessment | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Strength** | Empirically robust; directly applicable to product design |
| **Limitation** | "Libertarian paternalism" is contested — who decides what counts as a better choice? |
| **Project Tension** | Nudge ↔ Food Freedom: must design nudges that support autonomy, not restrict it |
