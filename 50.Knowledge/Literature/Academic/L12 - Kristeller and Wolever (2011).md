# L12 — Kristeller & Wolever (2011): Mindfulness-Based Eating Awareness Training (MB-EAT)

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Kristeller, J.L. and Wolever, R.Q. (2011) 'Mindfulness-based eating awareness training for treating binge eating disorder: the conceptual foundation', *Eating Disorders*, 19(1), pp. 49–61.

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** MB-EAT (Mindfulness-Based Eating Awareness Training) is a structured 9-week group intervention developed by Jean Kristeller in the 1990s and formalised in this 2011 paper. It adapts mindfulness meditation specifically for eating behaviour, training participants to attend to: (1) **hunger and satiety cues** — identifying physical sensations of hunger and fullness through guided meditation; (2) **taste and satisfaction** — noticing when food actually stops tasting as good (sensory-specific satiety); (3) **emotional triggers** — distinguishing physical hunger from emotional hunger. The 2011 paper reviews the conceptual foundation and clinical evidence from trials showing significant reduction in binge eating episodes, improvement in eating regulation, and reduced depression.

**So What:** MB-EAT is the most clinically validated programme for developing **interoceptive literacy** — the ability to read and trust one's body's signals about food. This directly operationalises the "Intuitive Technology" scenario's design goal: "AI amplifies internal body attunement rather than replaces it." However, MB-EAT has a critical limitation that is directly relevant to this project: it functions as a course, not a product. It has no persistent digital feedback layer, no ongoing prompts between sessions, and no mechanism for the insights gained in meditation to be connected to real-world eating data. As one design observation from the Shannon notes puts it: "any system without ongoing feedback is hard to sustain" — MB-EAT proves this empirically (high clinical efficacy, low population reach due to course format).

**Now What:** MB-EAT defines the **content** of what feedback should cultivate (hunger awareness, satiety awareness, emotional trigger awareness). This project's Intuitive Technology scenario could be framed as "the MB-EAT curriculum, delivered with the persistence and immediacy of Strava." Connects to [[L3 - Tribole and Resch (2020)]] (intuitive eating principles), [[L1 - Fogg (2009)]] (B=MAP: MB-EAT has no ongoing Prompt mechanism), and [[L7 - Kahneman (2011)]] (targeting System 1 bodily sensing vs System 2 deliberation).

## 🔑 Key Concepts & Design Implications

| Concept | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **MB-EAT structure** | 9-week group course; guided meditation + group discussion + home practice diary |
| **Core feedback mechanism** | Internal only — "your sensations are the data" |
| **Clinical efficacy** | Significant reduction in binge eating, improved eating regulation, reduced depression |
| **Critical design gap** | No digital persistence, no real-world data integration, no ongoing prompts |
| **Fogg B=MAP failure** | High Motivation (clinical setting) + some Ability + zero Prompt outside sessions → poor real-world retention |
| **Design opportunity** | Bridge MB-EAT interoceptive training with ambient digital feedback layer |
| **Interoceptive domains** | Hunger cues · satiety cues · taste satisfaction · emotional triggers |

## 📊 MB-EAT vs This Project's Design Goal

| Dimension | MB-EAT | This Project's Target |
|-----------|--------|-----------------------|
| **Feedback source** | Internal (meditation) | Internal + externally amplified |
| **Delivery format** | Weekly group sessions | Ambient, persistent, low-friction |
| **Data** | Subjective sensation | Sensation + optional biometric |
| **Prompt mechanism** | Class schedule only | Real-time, contextual |
| **Population reach** | Clinical / motivated | General consumer |
| **Risk of disordered eating** | Low (non-judgmental) | Must be maintained in design |

## 💡 Suggested Use in Proposal

> *"Kristeller and Wolever's (2011) MB-EAT programme demonstrates that interoceptive awareness — the ability to read and trust bodily signals about hunger, satiety, and emotional eating — can be trained. Its clinical efficacy is well-established. What it lacks is precisely what this project aims to provide: the persistent, ambient feedback layer that sustains the practice beyond the clinic. MB-EAT defines the goal; this project asks what design infrastructure could deliver it at scale."*
