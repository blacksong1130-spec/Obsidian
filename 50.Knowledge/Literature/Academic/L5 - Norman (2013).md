# L5 — Norman (2013): The Design of Everyday Things

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Norman, D.A. (2013) *The Design of Everyday Things* (Revised and expanded edn). New York: Basic Books.

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** Norman's revised design classic articulates fundamental principles of human-centred design: **feedback** (the system communicates what has happened), **conceptual models** (the user understands what the system does), and the **gulf of evaluation** (the user cannot determine the current state of the system). Applied across product, interface, and industrial design.

**So What:** Norman's feedback principle illuminates why dietary behaviour resists change: the human body is a black box with a 90-day reporting delay. People eating well experience a 'gulf of evaluation' so wide they cannot determine whether their actions are having any effect. This is a classic feedback failure — not a motivational failure.

**Now What:** Use Norman's feedback taxonomy to classify types of feedback dietary systems could provide, and to evaluate speculative prototypes against his design principles. The 'gulf of evaluation' concept is a powerful rhetorical tool for explaining the feedback gap. Connects to [[L1 - Fogg (2009)]] — the Prompt in B=MAP is essentially Norman's feedback signal.

## 🔑 Key Concepts & Design Implications

| Norman Concept | Application to Dietary Feedback |
|---------------|----------------------------------|
| **Feedback** | Body gives no immediate, interpretable signal after eating well |
| **Gulf of Evaluation** | 90-day metabolic delay = users cannot assess whether they're succeeding |
| **Conceptual Model** | Users have no mental model of how nutrition affects them day-to-day |
| **Affordance** | Healthy food has no behavioural affordance — it doesn't "invite" tracking |
| **Signifiers** | Current apps use numbers as signifiers — but numbers lack emotional resonance |

| Overall Assessment | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Strength** | Foundational; provides precise vocabulary for diagnosing design failures |
| **Limitation** | Written primarily for industrial/interface design; less developed on social/emotional dimensions |
| **Project Application** | "Closing the gulf of evaluation" = the core design brief of this entire project |
