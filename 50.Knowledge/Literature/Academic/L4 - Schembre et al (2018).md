# L4 — Schembre et al. (2018): Just-in-Time Feedback in Diet and Physical Activity

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Schembre, S.M., Liao, Y., Robertson, M.C., Dunton, G.F., Kerr, J., Haffey, M.E., Burnett, T., Basen-Engquist, K. and Hicklen, R.S. (2018) 'Just-in-time feedback in diet and physical activity interventions: systematic review and practical design framework', *Journal of Medical Internet Research*, 20(3), e106. https://doi.org/10.2196/jmir.8701

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** A systematic review and practical design framework for just-in-time (JIT) adaptive interventions in diet and physical activity. The review identifies key design principles for effective JIT feedback: **ecological momentary assessment**, **real-time data**, **personalisation**, and **appropriate timing of prompts**. Also documents widespread high drop-off rates in food tracking apps.

**So What:** This is the closest existing literature to the design approach this project proposes. JIT feedback — delivered at the right moment, in the right form, with appropriate emotional register — is precisely what current dietary apps fail to provide. The review's gap analysis directly supports the project's argument that dietary feedback is a profoundly underdesigned space.

**Now What:** Use the JIT framework as a structural model for designing dietary feedback systems in the speculative prototyping phase. The documented design gaps (no real-time dietary signal, poor emotional tenor, high friction) become the specific problems the speculative work aims to address. Connects to [[L1 - Fogg (2009)]] on Prompt design.

## 🔑 Key Conclusions & Design Implications

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Core Finding** | Most dietary feedback fails because it is not just-in-time — it's delayed, manual, and emotionally flat |
| **JIT Design Principles** | Real-time data · Personalisation · Right-moment delivery · Low friction |
| **Key Strength** | Comprehensive systematic review; practical design framework directly applicable |
| **Key Limitation** | Focuses on health behaviour technology; limited engagement with design aesthetics or emotional resonance |
| **Gap Identified** | No existing tool combines real-time dietary signal + emotional resonance + eating-disorder safety |
| **Project Application** | JIT principles = design specification for speculative prototypes |
