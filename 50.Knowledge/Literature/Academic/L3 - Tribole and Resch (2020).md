## What / So What / Now What

**What:** Intuitive Eating is a framework developed by two registered dietitians that rejects external dietary rules (calorie counting, macros, food morality) in favour of **internal attunement** to hunger, fullness, and satisfaction. The fourth edition synthesises over 150 studies supporting the approach's effectiveness for psychological wellbeing, eating behaviour, and body image.

**So What:** Essential for the ethical constraint this project must navigate. Any dietary feedback system that works *against* intuitive eating principles — imposing external targets, creating food guilt, or bypassing internal hunger signals — risks causing harm. Tribole and Resch's framework defines what a safe and empowering feedback system must preserve.

**Now What:** Use the ten principles of intuitive eating as a design evaluation rubric: Does the proposed system honour hunger and fullness? Does it make peace with food? Does it discover the satisfaction factor? These become non-negotiable design constraints in the speculative prototyping phase. Connects to [[L6 - Roth et al (2024)]] on eating disorder risk.

## 🔑 Key Principles & Design Implications

| Intuitive Eating Principle                 | Design Constraint for This Project                  |
| ------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Reject the Diet Mentality                  | No weight-loss framing in feedback                  |
| Honour Your Hunger                         | System must not override internal hunger signals    |
| Make Peace with Food                       | No "good food / bad food" binary in any feedback    |
| Challenge the Food Police                  | Eliminate guilt-inducing notifications or scoring   |
| Discover the Satisfaction Factor           | Feedback should reinforce pleasure, not diminish it |
| Feel Your Fullness                         | Design should amplify, not replace, body attunement |
| Cope with Your Emotions without Using Food | System must not weaponise emotional states          |
| Respect Your Body                          | No body-shaming metrics or comparisons              |
| Movement — Feel the Difference             | Feedback should feel good, not punitive             |
| Honour Your Health with Gentle Nutrition   | Nutrition guidance, not nutrition surveillance      |

| Overall Assessment | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Strength** | Empirically supported; defines what "harm-free" design looks like |
| **Limitation** | Primarily a clinical/therapeutic framework — needs translation to product design |
