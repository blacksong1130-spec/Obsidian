# P6 — Near Future Laboratory (2014): TBD Catalog

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Near Future Laboratory (2014) *TBD Catalog: Things From the Near Future* [Design fiction]. Available at: https://nearfuturelaboratory.com/projects/tbd-catalog/ (Accessed: 6 April 2026).

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** TBD Catalog is a design fiction project presenting a mail-order catalog of products from the near future: quantified-self devices, smart home appliances, and personal health tools framed as mundane consumer products. The catalog aesthetic makes the speculative feel **ordinary and purchasable**, defamiliarising the present by showing how current trends will look when normalised.

**So What:** The catalog includes speculative dietary and health tracking items directly relevant to this project. More broadly, its methodology offers a communication approach for speculative artefacts: presenting future dietary feedback tools as if they were already products available for purchase — **mundane plausibility over sci-fi spectacle**.

**Now What:** Use the catalog format as a possible presentation mode for speculative prototypes. Mundane plausibility is more persuasive and provocative than spectacular futures — the TBD Catalog demonstrates this clearly.

---

## 🔑 Key Qualities & Design Lessons

| TBD Catalog Quality | Why It Matters | Application to This Project |
|--------------------|---------------|----------------------------|
| **Mundane framing** | Products are boring, familiar, ordinary-looking | Future dietary tools should feel like 2035 Amazon listings |
| **Consumer catalog format** | Normalises the speculative | Present dietary feedback artefacts as "already for sale" |
| **Near future** | 10–15 year horizon, not sci-fi | Realistic technology horizon matches this project's 2035 focus |
| **Product descriptions** | Dry, informational copy heightens the uncanny | Use product copy to reveal assumptions embedded in feedback design |
| **Quantified self items** | Some items directly address health tracking | Precedent for dietary feedback as consumer product category |

| Overall Assessment | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Key Strength** | Brilliant communication format; makes design fiction immediately accessible |
| **Key Limitation** | Humor and irony can undercut the seriousness of the design critique |
| **Design Lesson** | "What would the 2035 dietary feedback tool look like in a Target catalog?" is a generative design question |
