# P1 — Strava (2009): Run and Ride Training App

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Strava Inc. (2009) *Strava: Run and Ride Training* [Mobile application]. Available at: https://www.strava.com (Accessed: 6 April 2026).

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** Strava is the dominant fitness tracking app for runners and cyclists (100M+ users). Its core innovation is not GPS accuracy (available since the early 2000s) but the **social and gamification layer**: public segments, Kudos (social validation), personal records, leaderboards, and challenges — transforming solitary exercise into a continuous social performance.

**So What:** Strava is the primary positive design reference for this project. It demonstrates that **feedback design**, not sensor accuracy, is what drives sustained behaviour change. The segment leaderboard makes a routine run feel competitive and emotionally meaningful in the moment. This is precisely what dietary feedback conspicuously lacks.

**Now What:** Analyse Strava's design patterns and ask the dietary translation question: What is the nutritional 'segment'? What is the metabolic 'Kudos'? What is the dietary 'personal record'? These translation questions drive the speculative prototyping phase.

---

## 🔑 Key Design Patterns & Dietary Analogies

| Strava Feature | Design Principle | Possible Dietary Analogue |
|----------------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| **Segments** | Any road becomes a competition | "Longest streak of balanced meals this week" |
| **Kudos** | Social validation for effort | Friends react to your "great eating day" |
| **Leaderboard** | Public ranking creates stakes | Community nutrition challenges |
| **Personal Records** | Progress is personal and emotionally resonant | "Your best gut-health week ever" |
| **Challenges** | Time-limited goals create urgency | "30-day Mediterranean eating challenge" |

| Overall Assessment | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Key Strength** | Proof that social/emotional feedback layer transforms behaviour without changing underlying technology |
| **Key Limitation** | Competitive social layer could create anxiety in dietary context — risk of comparison harm |
| **Design Lesson** | The layer on top of measurement is more important than measurement accuracy itself |
