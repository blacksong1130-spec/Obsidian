# P9 — Woebot (2017): AI Mental Health Chatbot

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Woebot Health (2017) *Woebot: Your Mental Health Ally* [Mobile application]. Available at: https://woebothealth.com (Accessed: 6 April 2026).

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** Woebot is an AI-powered mental health chatbot delivering CBT techniques through conversational interaction. Users check in daily; Woebot responds with evidence-based techniques for managing anxiety, depression, and mood. It has demonstrated efficacy in randomised controlled trials for reducing depressive symptoms — making it one of the few AI health tools with clinical validation.

**So What:** Woebot is relevant as an analogue: it demonstrates that **AI-mediated conversational feedback** can produce clinically meaningful behaviour change in psychological domains. The design question: could a conversational, emotionally intelligent AI provide reflective, non-judgemental feedback about eating — at scale and in real time — in the way a skilled dietitian might?

**Now What:** Woebot's conversational model offers a template for one speculative scenario: an AI dietary companion that builds a **non-judgemental relationship** with the user's eating over time, without calorie counting. Connects to the 'Intuitive Technology scenario' in Section 5d.

---

## 🔑 Key Qualities & Design Lessons

| Woebot Quality | Why It Matters | Dietary Feedback Application |
|----------------|---------------|------------------------------|
| **Conversational interface** | Low friction; feels like texting a friend | Replace food log with conversation: "How did that meal feel?" |
| **Non-judgemental tone** | CBT framing avoids shame/guilt | Critical for eating-disorder-safe design |
| **Daily check-in ritual** | Builds habit loop without pressure | Morning dietary reflection as gentle ritual |
| **Emotional attunement** | Responds to mood, not just data | Dietary feedback sensitive to emotional state |
| **Clinical validation** | RCT evidence for efficacy | Demonstrates AI feedback can produce real behaviour change |
| **Scalability** | No human therapist required | Could scale to millions of users |

| Overall Assessment | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Key Strength** | Clinically validated proof that AI conversational feedback changes psychological behaviour |
| **Key Limitation** | Mental health ≠ dietary behaviour; transfer may not be straightforward |
| **Design Lesson** | "What would a dietary Woebot look like?" — a companion that talks about food with curiosity, not surveillance |
