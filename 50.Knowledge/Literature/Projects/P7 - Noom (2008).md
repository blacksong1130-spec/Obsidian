# P7 — Noom (2008): Healthy Weight Loss App

## 📖 Citation (Harvard)
Noom Inc. (2008) *Noom: Healthy Weight Loss* [Mobile application]. Available at: https://www.noom.com (Accessed: 6 April 2026).

---

## What / So What / Now What

**What:** Noom is a digital health app combining **cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT)** principles with food logging and human coaching. Its colour-coded food classification (green / yellow / red by caloric density) attempts to replace calorie arithmetic with a simpler, emotionally accessible system. It employs 3,000+ certified coaches and has empirical evidence for weight loss efficacy.

**So What:** Noom represents the most sophisticated existing attempt to apply behavioural psychology to dietary feedback, and its relative success vs. pure calorie-counting apps validates the B=MAP approach. However, it still operates within a **weight-loss framing** fundamentally at odds with food freedom principles.

**Now What:** Use Noom as a case study in the failure mode of well-intentioned dietary design: it gets the psychology right but the framing wrong. The speculative question: what would Noom look like if designed for **food freedom** rather than weight loss? Connects to [[L3 - Tribole and Resch (2020)]] on intuitive eating principles.

---

## 🔑 Strengths, Limitations & Design Lessons

| Dimension | Noom's Approach | Verdict |
|-----------|----------------|---------|
| **Theoretical basis** | CBT + behaviour change psychology | ✅ Stronger than pure calorie counting |
| **Food classification** | Green / Yellow / Red colour coding | ⚠️ Simpler than numbers but still "good food / bad food" binary |
| **Human coaching** | Real coaches available | ✅ High emotional support; not scalable |
| **Goal framing** | Weight loss as primary goal | ❌ Incompatible with food freedom / intuitive eating |
| **Eating disorder risk** | No explicit safeguards documented | ❌ Weight-loss framing increases disordered eating risk |
| **Social layer** | Group challenges, community | ⚠️ Can create comparison anxiety |

| Overall Assessment | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Key Strength** | Proves CBT-based dietary feedback produces better outcomes than pure tracking |
| **Key Limitation** | Weight-loss framing undermines the psychological sophistication of the CBT approach |
| **Design Lesson** | Psychology-first design + wrong framing = partial success; this project needs psychology-first + food-freedom framing |
